I am not a designer before- after I graduated college. I even forgot that arts and the creative side is my strength. That there is where I can say I excel even when I was still young. I almost forgot because I juggled school with work when I was in college. I have to be at work in the morning and be at school in the evening. Adobe apps aren't my friends back then. I can say I don't have the time to really practice.

After college, aside from my day job, I started accepting creating event invitations from people I know. And that is where it all started. I have to know how to edit and do layouts with photoshop. I noticed that although there are a lot of revisions from them, I can say that I enjoy what I do. And they liked it. People around me tell me why don't I apply as a designer if this is what I really want to to. I always say I want to but I think my skills aren't enough. It took me three years before deciding- to have the courage to make a leap from being a software QA to being a designer. I asked for clients to design for them pro bono for my portfolio. I am so glad I followed what they are telling me. Haha. I finally had the job that I enjoy doing.

I am still studying. I want to know more about UX and the research aside from just the mere putting myself in the feet of the users- the User Experience almost everybody know. I can't wait to learn more as a UI/UX designer and share more of it to you very soon.
